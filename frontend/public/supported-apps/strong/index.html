Strong

Strong is a great workout logger, but its built-in charts leave you guessing. LiftShift turns your Strong CSV export into detailed analytics — muscle heatmaps, plateau detection, PR tracking, and set-by-set feedback.

Import options

  • CSV import — Export your workout history from Strong and upload the file to LiftShift. All analysis runs locally in your browser.

How to export your Strong data

  1. Open the Strong app on your phone.
  2. Go to Profile → Export Data.
  3. Choose CSV as the export format.
  4. Save or share the file to your computer.
  5. Open LiftShift, click the import button, and select the CSV file.

Strong CSV exports can vary in format. LiftShift handles the most common variants automatically, including semicolon-delimited files and unit-suffixed headers.

What LiftShift reads from your Strong export

  • Exercise names — Normalised so variations like “Bench Press” and “Barbell Bench” are recognised as the same movement.
  • Set reps and weight — Used for training volume, 1RM estimates, and PR detection.
  • Dates — Used for trend charts, consistency heatmaps, and rolling window comparisons.

Common Strong export variants

Strong exports can differ depending on your app version and device. LiftShift handles these automatically:

  • Semicolon-delimited files — Some versions of Strong use semicolons instead of commas as delimiters. LiftShift detects and parses both.
  • Unit-suffixed headers — Headers like “Weight (kg)” or “Weight (lb)” are recognised and stripped automatically.
  • Routine names as workout identifiers — If your Strong export includes routine names, LiftShift displays them in your workout history.

Troubleshooting

LiftShift shows an error on import

Make sure you exported as CSV, not another format. If the file has a .txt or .json extension, re-export and choose CSV.

Exercise names are split across multiple entries

Strong sometimes exports slight variations of the same exercise name (e.g., “Bench Press” vs “Bench Press (Barbell)”). LiftShift normalises many common variations automatically. If you find a case that isn’t handled, try renaming exercises consistently in Strong before exporting.

Switched from Strong to Hevy?

You can import data from both apps into LiftShift. The dashboard merges everything into one unified view. Exercise names are normalised across apps so your history stays clean. See the Supported apps page for other import guides.

What analytics you get with Strong data

  • Muscle heatmaps — See which muscles get the most (and least) weekly volume.
  • Smart PR tracking — All-time bests, 2-month bests, and premature PR flags.
  • Training volume — Graph your volume over weeks, months, or custom date ranges.
  • Exercise status labels (Getting stronger / Plateauing / Taking a dip) with confidence levels.
  • Set-by-set feedback analysing every set in any past workout.
